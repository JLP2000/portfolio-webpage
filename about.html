Motivation?

I first discovered coding during my time at University, the first language I ever learnt was Java in my first-year. I fell in love with the concept of programming instantly and wanted to learn more!

As I continued through my course, I continued to learn different coding languages and the working behind the languages such as C, 8051 Assembly language, Arduino. I really enjoyed learning these new languages and started to develop a passion for it

During my third year and final year project is when I decided I really wanted to pursue a career on the software side of tech. I noticed that the parts of the course that I found most interesting and enjoyable for myself was whenever I got to learn code or build and develop programs for my hardware.

But, as I started to look more into the tech industry and the skills required, I had realized that the languages that I had been taught were only the beginning and I needed to learn more. The programming languages and skills that I had learnt during my time at University were low-level languages. I needed to find a way where I could learn and build a strong foundation of skills that would allow me to enter the tech industry which is where I stumbled onto La Fosse Academy.

How?

After I graduated university, I started to self-learn Python through Youtube tutorials and w3schools, but soon hit a wall.

I realised I didn't know what skills employers were actually looking for and what should I be learning? How should I structure my files? What is Github?

I went on a 13-week tech bootcamp at La Fosse Academy in October 2022, where I learnt so many new skills and languages including HTML, CSS, Javascript, React, Redux, SQL, noSQL, and Python

During the bootcamp, I got to work with many great devs, get some experience working agile using Trello and Kanban. It was an amazing experience where I got to meet and work with amazing people and gain more experience working in group projects which made me more determined to build more full stack projects

Now.

On completion of the bootcamp, I have learnt many languages and skills and I now am looking for work within the tech industry in order to continue to learn and develop my skills and put what I have learn to some good use!

I hope to be able to find a position that will allow me to continue to learn about the tech industry and new technologies and gain more experience to continue to strengthen what I have learnt

I am constantly looking for new ways to level up my skill by completing mini-projects in my free-time to continue to learn more and keep myself sharp!
